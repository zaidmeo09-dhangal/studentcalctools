How This Grade Calculator Works

A grade calculator with weights helps you understand how each part of your course affects your overall grade.

Instead of treating every assignment the same, it gives more value to the work that counts more toward your final course result.

For example, a final exam worth 40% of your grade has a bigger impact than a homework assignment worth 10%. This calculator uses your score and grade weight for each item to calculate your weighted grade average.

POINTS TO PERCENTAGE

How to Use Scores Given as Points

Some teachers give scores as points instead of percentages. For example, you may get 45 out of 50 on a test, 18 out of 20 on a quiz, or 92 out of 100 on an exam.

Before adding that score to the calculator, convert the points into a percentage.

Formula:
Score Percentage = Points Earned ÷ Total Points × 100

Example:
45 ÷ 50 × 100 = 90%

After converting the score, enter 90% into the calculator and add the correct weight for that test, quiz, exam, assignment, or midterm.

This makes the calculator helpful for students who need a test grade calculator, quiz grade calculator, or exam grade calculator but already have their score as points.

WEIGHTED GRADES

Grade Calculator With Weights

Many classes do not calculate grades by simple average. A test may count more than homework, a final exam may count more than a quiz, and a project may count more than participation.

This grade calculator with weights helps you avoid guessing. Add each graded item, enter the percentage weight, and see how your scores combine into one current weighted grade.

Example:
Homework: 92% with 20% weight
Midterm: 84% with 30% weight
Final exam: 88% with 50% weight

The calculator combines those scores based on their weights to estimate your current weighted course grade.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a grade calculator?+
A grade calculator is a tool that helps you calculate your course grade from assignment scores, test scores, exam scores, and grade weights. It can show your weighted grade percentage and estimated letter grade.
How does a weighted grade calculator work?+
A weighted grade calculator multiplies each score by its weight, adds the weighted scores together, then divides by the total weight. This gives a more accurate result when some assignments count more than others.
Can I use this as a test grade calculator?+
Yes. You can use it as a test grade calculator by entering your test score as a percentage and adding the weight of the test. If your teacher gives points, convert your points into a percentage first.
Can I use this as an exam grade calculator?+
Yes. You can use it as an exam grade calculator by entering your exam percentage and the exam weight. This is useful when an exam, midterm, or final counts as a large part of your course grade.
How do I convert points into a percentage?+
Divide the points you earned by the total points possible, then multiply by 100. Example: 45 ÷ 50 × 100 = 90%. So, 45 out of 50 equals 90%.
Can I use this as a semester grade calculator?+
Yes. You can use it as a semester grade calculator by adding the major grades from your semester, including assignments, quizzes, tests, projects, midterms, and exams.
Do my grade weights need to add up to 100%?+
No. The calculator can still work if your weights do not add up to 100%. It uses the total weight you entered as the denominator.
What is the difference between a grade calculator and a final grade calculator?+
A grade calculator estimates your current weighted grade from the scores and weights you enter. A final grade calculator helps you find the score you need on your final exam to reach a target course grade.
What is the difference between a grade calculator and a GPA calculator?+
A grade calculator estimates your percentage grade for one class. A GPA calculator converts course grades into grade points and calculates your GPA across multiple classes.
Is this grade calculator free?+
Yes. This grade calculator is free to use. You do not need to create an account or sign up.
Is my data saved?+
No. Your calculations happen in your browser. Your scores are not stored or shared.
