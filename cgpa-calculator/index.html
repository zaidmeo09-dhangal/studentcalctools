FORMULA

How to Calculate Cumulative GPA

Cumulative GPA is calculated as a credit-weighted average across all semesters. Semesters with more credit hours have more impact on your final CGPA than semesters with fewer credits.

Formula:
Cumulative GPA = Total of Semester GPA × Semester Credits ÷ Total Credits

Example:

  • Semester 1 GPA: 3.40 with 15 credits
  • Semester 2 GPA: 3.70 with 18 credits
  • 3.40 × 15 = 51.00
  • 3.70 × 18 = 66.60
  • Total grade points = 117.60
  • Total credits = 33
  • Cumulative GPA = 117.60 ÷ 33 = 3.56

This is how to calculate cumulative GPA for all semesters when each semester has a different number of credit hours.

GPA TERMS

CGPA vs GPA

GPA and CGPA are related, but they are not always used the same way.

TermMeaning
GPAGrade Point Average for one semester, term, or grading period.
CGPACumulative Grade Point Average across multiple semesters.
Cumulative GPAAnother name for CGPA, commonly used in the U.S.
Semester GPAGPA earned in one semester only.

GPA usually looks at one term. CGPA or cumulative GPA looks at your overall academic performance across all completed semesters.

WEIGHTING

Is Cumulative GPA Weighted or Unweighted?

Cumulative GPA can be weighted in two different ways, so it is important to understand what your school means.

This calculator is credit-weighted. That means semesters with more credit hours count more than semesters with fewer credit hours.

This is not the same as weighted GPA for honors, AP, IB, or advanced classes. If your school gives weighted GPA points for course difficulty, enter the official semester GPA shown on your transcript or grade report.

PERCENTAGE

CGPA Calculator to Percentage

Some students need to convert CGPA to percentage for applications, transcripts, or international forms.

This calculator focuses on calculating CGPA from semester GPA and credits. It does not apply one universal CGPA to percentage formula because schools and countries use different conversion rules.

Simple scale example:
CGPA Percentage = CGPA ÷ GPA Scale × 100

If your CGPA is 3.6 out of 4.0, then 3.6 ÷ 4.0 × 100 = 90%. This is only a simple scale estimate. Official percentage conversion may be different.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a cumulative GPA calculator?+
A cumulative GPA calculator is a tool that calculates your overall GPA across multiple semesters. It uses each semester GPA and the credit hours completed during that semester.
What is a CGPA calculator?+
A CGPA calculator helps you calculate your Cumulative Grade Point Average. CGPA is the average of your academic performance across all completed semesters, usually weighted by credit hours.
Is CGPA the same as cumulative GPA?+
Yes. CGPA and cumulative GPA usually mean the same thing. CGPA is short for Cumulative Grade Point Average, while cumulative GPA is the phrase commonly used in many U.S. schools and colleges.
How do I calculate CGPA?+
Multiply each semester GPA by the credits for that semester. Add those results together, then divide by the total credits from all semesters.
Can I use this as a cumulative GPA calculator for college?+
Yes. College students can use this calculator to estimate cumulative GPA across completed semesters, as long as they know their semester GPA and credit hours.
Can I use this as a CGPA calculator out of 4?+
Yes. If your school uses a 4.0 GPA scale, enter each semester GPA out of 4.0 and the related credit hours.
Can this calculator convert CGPA to percentage?+
This calculator does not apply an official CGPA to percentage formula because conversion rules vary by school and country. If needed, use your institution’s official conversion scale.
Why do credit hours matter?+
Credit hours matter because semesters with more credits have more impact on your cumulative GPA. A larger credit load affects your GPA more than a smaller credit load.
Is my data saved?+
No. Your calculations happen in your browser. Your GPA, credits, and semester details are not stored or shared.
