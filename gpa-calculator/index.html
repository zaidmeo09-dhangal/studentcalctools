Each letter grade has a grade point value on the 4.0 scale. Your GPA is calculated by converting each letter grade into grade points, then averaging those points based on credit hours.

FORMULA

How to Calculate GPA

GPA is calculated by converting each course grade into grade points, multiplying those points by the course credits, adding the results, then dividing by total credits.

Formula: GPA = Total Grade Points ÷ Total Credits

Example: English: A, 3 credits. Math: B+, 4 credits. History: A-, 3 credits.

A = 4.0 × 3 credits = 12.0. B+ = 3.3 × 4 credits = 13.2. A- = 3.7 × 3 credits = 11.1. Total grade points = 36.3. Total credits = 10. GPA = 36.3 ÷ 10 = 3.63.

PERCENTAGE TO GPA

GPA Calculator From Percentage

If your teacher gives grades as percentages, convert the percentage into a letter grade first. For example, 92% usually maps to A-, 85% usually maps to B, and 78% usually maps to C+.

After choosing the closest letter grade, enter that grade into the calculator with the course credits. This makes the tool useful as a GPA calculator from percentage when your school uses percentage grades but reports GPA on a 4.0 scale.

GPA TYPES

Weighted vs Unweighted GPA

This calculator uses the standard 4.0 GPA scale and credit hours. That means it is credit-weighted because courses with more credits count more toward your GPA.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a GPA calculator?+
A GPA calculator is a tool that estimates your Grade Point Average using your course grades and credit hours. It converts letter grades into grade points and calculates your GPA on the 4.0 scale.
How do you calculate GPA?+
To calculate GPA, multiply each course’s grade points by its credit hours. Add all grade points together, then divide by total credits. Formula: GPA = Total Grade Points ÷ Total Credits.
Can I use this as a high school GPA calculator?+
Yes. High school students can use this GPA calculator to estimate GPA for a semester or grading period. If your school uses weighted GPA for honors, AP, or IB classes, check your school’s official scale.
Can I use this as a college GPA calculator?+
Yes. College students can use this calculator to estimate semester GPA by entering each course, letter grade, and credit hours.
Can I use this as a GPA calculator from percentage?+
Yes. If you have percentage grades, convert each percentage into the closest letter grade first. Then enter the letter grade and course credits into the calculator.
Can I use this GPA calculator without credits?+
Yes. If your school does not use credits, enter the same credit value for each course, such as 1 credit per class. This gives each class equal weight.
Is this a weighted GPA calculator?+
This calculator is credit-weighted because it uses course credits. It is not a full honors, AP, or IB weighted GPA calculator unless your school’s official grades already reflect those weighted values.
Is this an unweighted GPA calculator?+
Yes. You can use it as an unweighted GPA calculator by entering standard 4.0 scale letter grades. If every course has the same credit value, each class will count equally.
What is a good GPA?+
A 3.0 GPA is usually considered good, a 3.5 GPA is strong, and a 3.7 or higher is often considered excellent. The meaning of a good GPA can vary by school, college, major, scholarship, or program.
What is the difference between GPA and CGPA?+
GPA usually refers to one semester or one group of courses. CGPA, or cumulative GPA, combines GPA across multiple semesters.
Why do credits matter in GPA?+
Credits matter because they decide how much each course affects your GPA. A 4-credit course has more impact than a 1-credit course.
Is my data saved?+
No. Your calculations happen in your browser. Your course names, grades, credits, and GPA results are not stored or shared.
