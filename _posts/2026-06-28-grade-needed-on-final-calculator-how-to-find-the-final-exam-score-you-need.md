## **The Formula for the Grade You Need on Your Final**

Here is the formula:

Required Final Exam Score = (Target Grade - Current Grade × (1 - Final Exam Weight)) ÷ Final Exam Weight

The final exam weight must be written as a decimal.

So:


| Final Exam Weight | Use in Formula |
| ----------------- | -------------- |
| 10% | 0.10 |
| 15% | 0.15 |
| 20% | 0.20 |
| 25% | 0.25 |
| 30% | 0.30 |
| 40% | 0.40 |
| 50% | 0.50 |


The formula works because your final course grade has two main parts:

1. The grade you already earned before the final
2. The grade you earn on the final exam

If your current grade counts for 80% of the course and your final exam counts for 20%, the formula calculates what final exam score is needed inside that remaining 20%.



## **Simple Example: What Grade Do You Need on the Final?**

Let’s say you have these numbers:


| Input | Value |
| ----------------- | ----- |
| Current Grade | 84% |
| Target Grade | 90% |
| Final Exam Weight | 25% |


Now plug those into the formula.

Required Final Exam Score = (90 - 84 × (1 - 0.25)) ÷ 0.25

First, subtract the final exam weight from 1:

1 - 0.25 = 0.75

Then multiply your current grade by 0.75:

84 × 0.75 = 63

Now subtract that from your target grade:

90 - 63 = 27

Finally, divide by the final exam weight:

27 ÷ 0.25 = 108

So, you would need **108% on the final exam** to finish the class with a 90%.

That means a 90% overall grade may not be possible through the final exam alone unless your class allows extra credit, drops a low score, rounds grades, or uses another grading policy.



## **Another Example: A More Achievable Final Exam Score**

Now let’s use a slightly lower target.


| Input | Value |
| ----------------- | ----- |

Calculate your current grade portion:

Subtract from the target:

Divide by the final exam weight:

You need **96% on the final exam** to finish with an 87%.

## **Why Final Exam Weight Changes Everything**

The final exam weight is one of the most important numbers in the calculation. A final worth 10% has limited power. A final worth 40% can seriously move your course grade.

Here is a simple comparison.

Assume:

- Current grade: 82%
- Target grade: 88%


| Final Exam Weight | Score Needed on Final |
| ----------------- | --------------------- |
| 10% | 142% |
| 20% | 112% |
| 30% | 102% |
| 40% | 97% |
| 50% | 94% |


Same current grade. Same target grade. Very different final exam scores.

That is why you should not calculate this from memory or vibes. Use the exact final exam weight from your syllabus, teacher instructions, or course portal.

Some schools also explain that final grades may be based on multiple assessment types, such as tests, essays, assignments, projects, participation, labs, and final exams. The key point is that the percentage contribution of each item matters, so the final exam weight should come from the official course grading breakdown, not guesswork. You can see this kind of grading structure discussed in university assessment policy guidance from  [HKUST]([https://registry.hkust.edu.hk/resource-library/assessment-policies-and-procedures](https://registry.hkust.edu.hk/resource-library/assessment-policies-and-procedures)) .



## **What If You Need More Than 100% on the Final?**

If the calculator shows a required score above 100%, your target grade may not be possible with a normal final exam score.

For example, if the result says you need 112%, even a perfect 100% may not be enough to reach your target.

This can happen when:

- Your current grade is too far below the target
- The final exam is not worth enough of the course grade
- Your target grade is very high
- Earlier assignments, quizzes, or tests carried too much weight
- Your course does not offer extra credit
- Your teacher does not round final grades

That does not always mean the situation is hopeless. Check your syllabus or ask your teacher whether any of these apply:


| Possible Policy | Why It Matters |
| -------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| Extra credit | May allow you to recover extra points |
| Dropped lowest score | Could raise your current grade before the final |
| Test corrections | May recover points before final grades close |
| Grade rounding | An 89.5% may round to 90% in some courses |
| Replacement final | Some classes replace a lower test score with the final |
| Curved grading | Your final score may be adjusted based on class performance |


Do not assume these rules exist. The formula gives the math answer. Your syllabus gives the official answer.

## **Common Mistakes Students Make When Calculating Final Exam Scores**

The formula is not hard, but small input mistakes can change the result a lot.


| Mistake | Why It Causes Problems |
| ------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Entering 25 instead of 0.25 in the manual formula | The formula requires decimal form |
| Using desired final exam score instead of target course grade | These are different numbers |
| Guessing the final exam weight | The weight must come from the syllabus or teacher |
| Using an outdated current grade | Missing assignments or ungraded work can change the result |
| Ignoring weighted categories | Tests, homework, labs, and projects may have different weights |
| Assuming rounding rules | Not every teacher rounds grades the same way |
| Forgetting extra credit rules | Extra credit can change what is possible |


The cleanest method is to use official numbers from your course system, then calculate from there.
